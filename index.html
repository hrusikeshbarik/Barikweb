About Me

I am Dr. Hrusikesh Barik, a Ph.D. graduate from IIT Bombay with expertise in Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) and numerical simulations of complex systems, particularly in fluid mechanics, heat and mass transfer, and gasification processes. My research focuses on clean coal utilization, pollutant reduction, and advanced simulation techniques to optimize combustion and multiphase flow systems.

Research Interests

  • Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD), Numerical Modelling and Simulation
  • Fluid Mechanics, Heat and Mass Transfer, Process Modelling and Optimization
  • Multiphase Flow, Combustion and Gasification
  • Data Science and Machine Learning in Energy

Work Experience

  • Project Associate, Sage (Sustainable Approach for Green Environment) (Jan 2021 - Aug 2024)

    At SAGE, I specialized in environmental system modeling and Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) analysis across various projects. I conducted building simulations to optimize ventilation, focusing on improving indoor air quality and energy efficiency through advanced airflow and heat transfer modeling. I also modeled pollutant dispersion in indoor environments to develop effective ventilation strategies for minimizing exposure to harmful substances. Additionally, I performed energy audits of buildings, analyzing HVAC systems to identify opportunities for reducing energy consumption and enhancing sustainability. My work extended to aerodynamic studies of vehicles using CFD, providing key insights for design improvements aimed at reducing drag and increasing fuel efficiency.
